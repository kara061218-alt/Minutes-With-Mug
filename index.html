What is Nexus?

A new kind of space for Sandringham students.

"A platform where curiosity meets community — and discussion leads to action."

Nexus is a digital space built exclusively for Sandringham College students. It's organised around your real interests — not your year level or classes. Join the groups that actually excite you, talk to people who think like you, and go deeper than a classroom ever allows.

Platform Safety

Safety is Our First Priority.

The Nexus admin team is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and responsible digital environment for every student at Sandringham College.

🛡️

Admin Commitment to Online Safety

The Nexus admin team places digital safety above everything else. We operate the platform in strict accordance with Australian government online safety guidelines — including the Online Safety Act 2021 and the eSafety Commissioner's frameworks for safe digital participation by young people.

🇦🇺   Aligned with the Online Safety Act 2021 & eSafety Commissioner Guidelines
🔒
Closed Community

Exclusive to Sandringham College students. Invite links are controlled and never made public. No anonymous or unverified members.

👁️
Active Admin Oversight

The admin team actively monitors the platform. All channels are observable. Issues are acted on promptly and escalated to school staff where required.

📋
Clear Community Guidelines

All members agree to explicit guidelines on joining — informed by eSafety Commissioner best-practice standards for student online communities.

👑
Accountable Group Leaders

Every group has a named student leader accountable for conduct, with admin oversight above them at all times.

🚨
Rapid Moderation Response

Harmful content, harassment, or policy violations are addressed swiftly. Discord's moderation tools combined with active admin intervention.

🤝
School-Staff Connection

Admins maintain a direct communication channel with Sandringham College staff. Serious concerns are immediately escalated.

✓

No harassment, bullying, or targeted behaviour of any kind — online or referencing offline interactions

✓

No sharing of personal information beyond first name and year level without explicit consent

✓

No content inappropriate for school-age students — all discussions are age-appropriate by design

✓

✓

Platform activity reviewable by school staff — transparency with the school is maintained at all times

Nexus is designed to align with the eSafety Commissioner's Safety by Design principles and the Australian Government's Online Safety Act 2021. We are committed to continuous improvement in line with national guidelines. Learn more at esafety.gov.au.

Teacher Involvement

Teachers Play Two Clear Roles.
Neither one is demanding.

Nexus is student-driven. Teachers don't run discussions or manage communities — but their support in two specific areas makes the platform stronger and safer for everyone.

01
🤝

Supporting External Collaboration

When a student group wants to connect with another school, an NPO, or an external organisation, the admin team prepares a clear, concise group proposal — an outline of who the group is, what they want to do, and why they want to connect.

The teacher's role is simply to co-sign and facilitate that connection — introducing the platform to the external party using the school's official voice.

  • Receives a ready-made proposal from the admin team — no preparation needed from the teacher
  • Reviews it briefly and approves or suggests minor adjustments
  • Helps make the initial contact with the external organisation or school
  • Is kept informed on how the collaboration develops
Admin prepares everythingTeacher lends credibility
02
🛡️

Responding to Reported Incidents

Every discussion group has a built-in reporting function. If a member flags inappropriate behaviour, it is routed through a clear process — with teacher involvement only at the final escalation step, if the situation genuinely warrants it.

In the vast majority of cases, the admin team handles everything independently. A teacher is only contacted when the issue needs school-level attention.

  • Student flags inappropriate behaviour via the group's report function
  • Admin team reviews and takes immediate action on Discord
  • Minor issues: resolved by admin — teacher is not contacted
  • Serious issues: admin informs the relevant teacher with a clear summary and recommendation
  • Teacher responds based on school policy — admin provides full context
Admin handles first responseTeacher only for serious cases
☕

A light touch — not a full-time commitment.

Teachers are partners in making Nexus safe and connected — but they are not expected to moderate discussions, read chat logs, or manage the community day-to-day. That's what the admin team is for. A teacher's involvement is occasional, purposeful, and always supported by everything the admin team has already prepared. Most of the time, no action is needed at all. The system is designed to be as light as possible on staff time, while keeping the school properly informed and protected.
